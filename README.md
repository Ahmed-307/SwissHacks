# Julius-Baer

## Challenge Title

**Reimagining Online and Mobile Banking in Private Banking**

## Introduction

### Problem Description

Julius Baer is continuously investing in the modernisation of its digital channels to complement its relationship-driven business model. Advanced online and mobile solutions already enable secure access, digital onboarding, and personalised services, but there is an opportunity to reimagine how clients interact with their wealth anytime, anywhere. This challenge asks teams to design the next wave of innovation for Julius Baer's online and mobile banking channels — strengthening the client experience while complementing relationship manager services and preserving the Bank's focus on trust, security, and personal advice.

### Case Introduction

Teams are asked to think beyond conventional eBanking, drawing inspiration from innovations in other industries, and to conceptualize and prototype digital solutions that enhance Julius Baer's online and mobile banking channels across the full client lifecycle — from onboarding and daily interaction to advisory and long-term wealth planning. As AI-powered capabilities multiply across the industry, solutions should also address the emerging challenge of navigating, integrating, and orchestrating a growing number of tools and intelligent functionalities in a coherent, client-centric way. The aim is a transformative client experience that supports — rather than replaces — the personal relationship with the relationship manager.

## Potential Users

Primary end users range from basic to sophisticated private-banking clients, together with relationship managers who remain central to the advisory model. The challenge is also relevant for internal product, digital-channel, and technology stakeholders evaluating how such solutions could fit into the existing ecosystem.

## Use Cases

Possible solutions may address, for example:

- **Augmented digital onboarding** that remains guided by the relationship manager while improving speed, convenience, and regulatory robustness.
- **Enhanced mobile and online experiences** that leverage existing capabilities (smartphone-only secure login, push authentication, portfolio overviews, trading, secure messaging) and extend them with new high-value features for sophisticated private clients.
- **Omnichannel interaction models** that integrate secure messaging, video, and chat across channels (app, web, and approved third-party messaging where applicable) to deepen two-way communication.
- **Digital tools for wealth insight** that translate complex portfolios into intuitive dashboards, alerts, and simulations tailored to high-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth needs.

## Expected Outcome

A conceptualized and prototyped digital solution that enhances Julius Baer's online and mobile banking channels across the client lifecycle. Deliverables should clearly articulate the client value proposition, demonstrate a functional or clickable prototype, and outline how the solution could be integrated into a private banking context.

Certain industry guardrails are out of scope (for example, the use of IBANs, the payment process, and KYC regulation).

Presentation format: presentation and demo. Key elements include clear problem articulation; a clear representation of the proposed mobile/digital experience; main functional highlights; an explanation of how the solution addresses the challenge; and visual components such as screens, journeys, diagrams, or charts where useful. Presentations should be concise and comprehensive, with short descriptions where necessary.

## Technology

### Available Technology

Additional data sources that may be leveraged by participants include publicly available financial market data, UX/UI design benchmarks, and open datasets relevant to digital banking and client interaction patterns.

### Expected or Suggested Tech Stack

No specific stack is required. Participants are free to use any tech stack or hardware to reimagine online and mobile banking.

### Additional Information

Certain industry guardrails are out of scope and should be treated as fixed (for example, the use of IBANs, the payment process, and KYC regulation). Teams are encouraged to be creative and to consider what can be changed versus these guardrails.

## Judging Criteria

| Criterion | Description | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Client-Centric Innovation | Degree to which the solution addresses real private-banking client needs and differentiates Julius Baer's digital offering | 25% |
| User Experience and Design | Quality of interaction design across online and mobile channels, clarity of information, and accessibility | 25% |
| Technical and Operational Feasibility | Realism of implementation within banking architecture, including security, scalability, and compliance | 25% |
| Strategic Impact | Potential to strengthen Julius Baer's position as a modern, tech-enabled wealth manager while preserving the central role of the relationship manager | 25% |

**Optional bonus:** submissions may receive additional recognition for relationship-enhancing digital tools that help relationship managers deliver more proactive, personalized, and data-informed advice without diluting the personal relationship; next-generation secure access and authentication that builds on or reimagines concepts such as smartphone-only secure login, push authentication, and multi-device usage while keeping a frictionless journey; or insight-driven dashboards and alerts that let clients monitor portfolios, receive tailored notifications, and act quickly across markets and asset classes.
